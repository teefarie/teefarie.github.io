Data Driven Design Process

Our entire process involves brainstorming, defining, prototyping and then developing your idea.

Part 1 - Collective Brainstorming

We speak with clients concerning their idea. This is a conversation concerning the target user, expected user interactions within the app as well as the business’ pain points.

Part 2 - UX Research, User Journey, and Storyboarding

A team placed on the project, breaking down the solution into user journeys and sketches. We lay out ideas on how the product should work and look. At this stage, our design lead hires a product designer to create a prototype.

Part 3 - Wireframing and Prototyping

The product team creates an interactive prototype which is shared with the client, development team and quality assurance for further clarification. Feedback is received with more content from the client and functional input from the development team.

Part 4 - User Testing

The User Experience Researcher tests an interactive prototype with potential users. Feedback is sent to the product designer, developers and project manager.

Part 5 - High Fidelity Design and Acceptance Criteria

The product designer reviews the final prototype while the project manager creates achievable sprints for the software developers and testers. The testers create a test plan and work with the project manager on acceptance criteria.

Part 6 - Pushed to Development

Software Development starts. The project manager holds a daily standup via chat and weekly milestones are communicated with the client.

De-risked Product Development

We have designed our product development process to be simple yet flexible enough to respond to changing priorities.

  • We break down each project into Epics. These are smaller modules that we arrange into achievable sprints of 2 weeks.

  • We create a delivery timeline approved by the client while coordinating daily standups to keep each member of the team up-to-date.

  • The Project Manager on every project communicates weekly milestone achievements to the client, ensuring we’re on the same page

  • Work done from daily builds is pushed to a test-engineer who assures it is up to our acceptance criteria.
